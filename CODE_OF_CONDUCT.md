# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## 1. Our Pledge
We are committed to providing a friendly, safe, welcoming, and collaborative environment for all team members, regardless of their location, timezone, cultural background, or experience level. 

As a distributed team of local and offshore resources, our success relies on mutual respect, clear communication, and shared technical accountability across all layers of our application.

## 2. Shared Standards
We hold ourselves to high professional standards across all dimensions of our engineering ecosystem:

*   **Frontend & Application**: We commit to writing clean, performant, and accessible code. We respect user experience guidelines and thorough UI/UX alignment.
*   **Backend & Database**: We prioritize data integrity, efficient queries, data privacy, and secure data handling practices.
*   **Third-Party Integrations**: We ensure external APIs and integrations are handled securely, errors are caught gracefully, and system dependencies are well-documented.

## 3. Distributed Team Expectations
Working across different countries and timezones requires specific behavioral commitments:

*   **Asynchronous Communication**: We write detailed pull request descriptions, documentation, and issue updates so team members can work independently without waiting for real-time answers.
*   **Cross-Cultural Respect**: We use simple, universal, and unambiguous language. We avoid regional slang, idioms, or cultural references that might isolate or confuse teammates.
*   **Collaboration Over Blame**: When system issues, bugs, or integration failures occur, we focus entirely on the root cause and the fix, never on personal blame.
*   **Respecting Time Zones**: We remain mindful of our teammates' working hours. We do not expect immediate responses outside of an individual’s standard working day, except in designated emergency scenarios.

## 4. Unacceptable Behavior
The following behaviors are considered violations of our team's standards:
*   Discriminatory, derogatory, or exclusionary language and behavior.
*   Public or private harassment, including microaggressions or condescending review comments.
*   Bypassing code review processes, security protocols, or testing requirements.
*   Publishing confidential data, proprietary source code, or internal credentials in public spaces.

## 5. Enforcement Responsibilities
Team leads and project managers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of behavior. They will take fair and appropriate corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## 6. Reporting and Escalation
Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project supervisor or designated HR contact. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly, and the privacy of the reporting party will be strictly respected.
